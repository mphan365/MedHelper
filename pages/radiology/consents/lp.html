Lumbar Puncture Consent

    Indications

      Back pain (interveretebral disc disease) +/- sciatica
      Post surgical back pain

    Contraindications

      Coagulopathy
      Allergy to steroid/local anaesthetic
      Pregnancy

    Pre-Procedure

      Informed consent
      Check previous imaging, ensure previous CT/MRI is available
      Check for coagulopathy or anticoagulants (as per anticoagulant protocol)
      Coagulation profile not routinely required

    Procedure

      Prone position in CT scanner
      Preliminary CT for planning
      Aseptic technique
      Local anaesthetic to skin
      Insertion of 22G spinal needle to appropriate lumbar epidural space, with injection of air to confirm position
      Injection 4mg dexamethasone and 1ml 1% lignocaine

    Post Procedure

      Clinical review within 30 minutes

    Indications

      Temporary increase in back pain / sciatica
      Minimal or no symptom improvement
      Dural puncture
      Intrathecal injection, which can very rarely cause arachnoiditis
      Epidural haematoma (very rare)
      Infection / meningitis / epidural abscess (rare)
